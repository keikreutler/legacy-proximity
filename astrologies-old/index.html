Akira's Butterfly Chair

This constellation of satellites appears twice yearly over the European Union.

Resembling a Butterfly Chair, observers noted how the the famous design from 1938 seems to be strained and lopsided, nearly about to break.

Fanciful spectators noted that a figure too grown for this chair's fragile frame may be trying to sit. Such jests, followed by a series of memes featuring the popular manga character Akira trying to sit in a Butterfly Chair, eventually lead to the constellation being popularly known as Akira's Butterfly Chair.

When this arrangement forms twice a year over the European Union, it's suggested something may be close to breaking, or not equipped for its circumstance, but that new, evolutionary understandings may come from it.
